Pop

Pop music is a genre characterized by its catchy melodies, simple lyrics, and mass appeal. It often incorporates elements from various genres, including rock, dance, and electronic music. Pop songs are typically structured with verses and choruses and aim to be easily accessible and enjoyable to a wide audience.

Hip-hop

Focuses on rhythmic vocal delivery (rapping), often accompanied by beats and sampling. It explores themes of street life, identity, and social issues.

R&B

A soulful genre blending jazz, gospel, and blues influences, often focusing on smooth vocals, emotional themes, and groovy rhythms.

Rock

Characterized by electric guitars, strong rhythms, and energetic performances. It includes subgenres like classic rock, alternative, and punk.

K-pop

K-pop is a popular music genre from South Korea, blending various styles like pop, hip-hop, and R&B. It's known for energetic performances, catchy tunes, synchronized dances, and strong fan engagement. K-pop groups like BTS and BLACKPINK have gained worldwide popularity.

Jazz

A genre rooted in improvisation, complex harmonies, and syncopated rhythms. It incorporates elements of blues, swing, and bebop, often featuring brass and wind instruments.

OPM

OPM (Original Pilipino Music) refers to music created by Filipino artists, featuring various genres like pop, rock, and ballads. It reflects Filipino culture and experiences and has gained popularity both locally and internationally.

Classical

Classical music is a genre known for its complex compositions, orchestral arrangements, and structured forms. It spans several periods, including Baroque, Classical, and Romantic, with a focus on melody, harmony, and intricate instrumentation.

Soul

A blend of rhythm and blues and gospel, soul music emphasizes emotional, powerful vocal performances with themes of love, life, and hardship.

Country

Originating in the American South, country music blends folk, Western, and blues, often telling stories of love, life, and hardship, with instruments like the guitar, banjo, and fiddle.
